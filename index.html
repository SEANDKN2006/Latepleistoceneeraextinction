Exploring Earth's Last Great Ice Age

Welcome to the Pleistocene

The Late Pleistocene was a time of dramatic environmental changes, megafauna, and early human migration. This epoch, part of the Quaternary Period, witnessed the last glacial period (often called the "Ice Age") and the subsequent transition to the current Holocene interglacial.

During this period, Earth's climate fluctuated between colder glacial periods and warmer interglacials, with massive ice sheets covering much of North America and Eurasia. These environmental changes had profound effects on the evolution and distribution of plants, animals, and early humans.

Why Study the Late Pleistocene?

Understanding the Late Pleistocene helps us comprehend:

  • How species respond to rapid climate change
  • The causes and consequences of megafauna extinctions
  • Human adaptation to extreme environments
  • The origins of modern ecosystems
  • Patterns of biodiversity loss and conservation

This period serves as an important analog for understanding current and future climate change scenarios.
