Chronology and Climate

Time Period

The Late Pleistocene spans from approximately 126,000 to 11,700 years ago, ending with the transition to the Holocene epoch.

Glacial Cycles

The epoch was characterized by repeated glacial cycles (cold periods with extensive ice sheets) and interglacials (warmer periods). The Last Glacial Maximum occurred about 26,500-19,000 years ago.

Sea Levels

During glacial maxima, sea levels were up to 120 meters lower than today, exposing land bridges like Beringia (between Asia and North America) and the Sunda Shelf (in Southeast Asia).

Major Events Timeline

126,000 years ago

Beginning of the Late Pleistocene. Eemian interglacial period (warmer than present).

115,000 years ago

Start of the last glacial period (Würm glaciation in Europe, Wisconsin glaciation in North America).

70,000-60,000 years ago

Modern humans begin migrating out of Africa.

26,500-19,000 years ago

Last Glacial Maximum - ice sheets at their greatest extent.

16,000-13,000 years ago

Major deglaciation begins. Bering land bridge floods around 11,000 years ago.

11,700 years ago

End of Pleistocene, beginning of Holocene epoch.

Environmental Characteristics

Glacial landscapes dominated northern continents

The mammoth steppe was a vast grassland ecosystem

Vegetation

Tundra and steppe vegetation dominated northern regions, with forests restricted to refugia. The "mammoth steppe" was an extensive grassland ecosystem supporting megafauna.

Atmosphere

CO₂ levels were around 180-200 ppm during glacial maxima (compared to 280 ppm pre-industrial and 415+ ppm today).

Temperature

Global average temperatures were 4-7°C cooler than today during glacial maxima, with greater cooling at high latitudes.
